The Book the CIA Classified — Pole Shifts, Resets, and the Story We Were Never Meant to Hear

In 1966, a man named Dr. Chan Thomas dropped a theory so disturbing the CIA classified it the same year.

It wasn’t about politics. It wasn’t about war. It was about the end of the world.

Not someday. Not in centuries. But in hours.

Thomas claimed the Earth suffers catastrophic resets every 6,500 years. Entire continents ripped from their moorings. Oceans smashing over mountains. Winds roaring at 1,000 miles per hour. A planet-wide crustal slip that erases civilizations in a single day.

And then, silence. The book — The Adam and Eve Story — vanished from shelves. For decades, it existed only as whispers and rumors.

Until 2013.

After a FOIA request, the CIA released 57 pages. No explanations. No missing chapters. Just diagrams, chilling predictions, and one haunting refrain:

We are overdue.

The Strange Man Behind the Theory

Thomas wasn’t a basement crank. He was an electrical engineer who worked on missile guidance systems, UFO propulsion, and ESP research. He could speak the language of both science and mysticism — and he used it to paint an apocalyptic picture of Earth as a death trap on a timer.

His ideas weren’t entirely new. Charles Hapgood had suggested crustal displacement in the 1950s — an idea even Einstein said was “conceivable.” But Thomas didn’t hedge. He gave a number. He gave a mechanism. And he gave a date that unnervingly brushed up against the biblical timeline for Noah’s Flood.

Evidence… or Myth?

Mainstream geology dismisses the idea. No global signature of such a cataclysm exists. But Thomas pointed to ancient flood myths from nearly every culture:

  • Noah in the Bible
  • Gilgamesh in Mesopotamia
  • Vishnu in Hindu lore
  • Nu‘u in Hawaiian tradition

He claimed they weren’t allegories — they were memories.

He tied in the erosion marks on the Sphinx, the ice-free Antarctic coast shown in the Piri Reis map, and underwater ruins like Yonaguni. He suggested a single, ancient, global civilization that knew the resets came — and tried to warn us.

The Biblical Connection No One Talks About

Strip away the pseudoscience, and something unsettling remains: the Bible itself describes resets. But it frames them not as natural cycles… but as judgments.

Genesis 1:2’s “formless and void” may describe a re-creation after a previous world was destroyed. Isaiah hints at a cosmic rebellion before Eden. 2 Peter warns that the next cleansing will not be with water, but with fire.

If Thomas’s “pole shift” theory is even partially right, it may only be the shadow of something far older and far stranger — a war behind the crust, a rebellion older than humanity, and a planet that has already died more than once.

What If the Past Is the Key?

If ancient myths are memories…
If the resets are real…
If Eden itself was a guarded outpost on a hostile world…

Then we are not living in the “first age” of humanity.
We are living in the latest.

And if the pattern holds, this age has an expiration date.
